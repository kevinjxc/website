Kevin Chen is a student at Cleveland STEM High School in Seattle, Washington. During his time at Cleveland, he completed eight of nine Advanced Placement (AP) courses at his school, accomplishing a 4.5/4.0 GPA. His School of Engineering & Design pathway heavily involves computer science, correlating with his projects in various applications. Additionally, he has been recognized in various other fields, with recognition for his strong interest in literature and his dedication to culturally-responsive leadership.

Kevin's ventures revolve around contributing his knowledge to support local communities and acting to resolve global issues. Particularly, he is passionate about societal implications of education, as access to education has the capacity to polarize society. Beliefs, decisions, and lifetime outcomes all depend on the level of education one gains. Kevin is committed to helping South Seattle students from diverse backgrounds gain exposure to STEM education, especially as he reflect on similar barriers in his past.

Kevin holds relevant experience in web and software development from his work in computer science. His previous projects have involved Java, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, C# (in Unity Engine), and more. Aside from this website, Kevin's work in software includes the UpSTREAM Program website, games such as 30+ and Spellling Bee, and applications such as One. In his spare time, Kevin can be found writing, freelance trumpeting, and photographing while traveling with his family throughout the Pacific Northwest.​
